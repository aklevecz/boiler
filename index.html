Krakow's DIY Club Community

Krakow’s electronic music scene today is small yet strong, characterised by a community of DIY promoters and collectives running parties out of intimate venues and basements. Boiler Room and Ballantine’s visited the picturesque Polish city to gauge how the promoters, artists and venue owners from this community are attempting to help this scene grow. As with any city, Krakow’s DIY club scene is not without its issues, be it lack of dedicated local press, gentrification caused by mass tourism or international focus heavily geared to larger scale events like festivals What is evident is Krakow’s clubbing community has a determination to work together to help their scene and its many talented artists, producers and DJs grow and develop. Stay tuned for the next part of our Boiler Room x Ballantine’s True Music Moscow 2019 project, where we’ll be going back to work with these communities and help shape the future of the scene.

Soft Boy Forever: An Irish Hip Hop Story

The film documents the group working out of bedrooms and makeshift studios, their music covering topics from the Repeal the 8th Movement, gentrification in Dublin and the pressure on Irish men to live up to the traditional idea of masculinity. Soft Boy Forever explores the mentality that has brought them together: not to harden up against the issues they face in a modern Ireland, but to ‘stay soft.’ They are the future of Irish music and this is Dublin today. The documentary features some of the most exciting young Irish music talent at the moment, including: Kojaque, Kean Kavanagh, Luka Palm, Gaptoof, Brièn, Matt Finnegan, Five to Two, Henry Earnest, Wastefellow, jar jar jr.
